Analysis 1

Why scores are stuck at ~46%

The formula is (0.7 × semantic + 0.3 × keyword) × 100.

The maximum achievable keyword score is 50% (substring matches only — no skill can exactly equal a full requirement sentence). So the ceiling from keyword alone is 0.3 × 0.5 = 15 points.

To hit 90%, you need: 0.9 = 0.7 × semantic + 0.15 → semantic must be ~107% — impossible.

Realistically, 75–80% is the ceiling for this job posting's requirements unless you game it perfectly.

Why dadidelux is #1 despite 95+ skills

Paradoxically, 95+ skills is hurting the score. Semantic score = average similarity of all your skills to the requirements. Skills like Data Pipelines, Airflow, ETL Processes have near-zero similarity to a software engineering role — they drag the mean down.

Musashi has the right types of skills (JavaScript (ES6+), TypeScript, React) but the (ES6+) suffix breaks the substring match — "javascript (es6+)" is not found inside "strong proficiency in javascript, typescript, and react", so keyword stays at 13%.
